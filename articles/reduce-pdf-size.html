How to Reduce PDF Size Without Losing Quality

5 Min Read • Updated March 2026

Large PDF files can be a headache. They're slow to upload, hard to email, and eat up storage space. But compressing them often results in blurry images and unreadable text. Here is how to strike the perfect balance.

Understanding PDF Compression

PDF compression works by removing redundant data and reducing the resolution of images. The trick is to remove enough data to shrink the file size without degrading the visual quality noticeably.

Lossless Compression

Shrinks the file without deleting any data. It's perfect for text-heavy documents but offers modest size reductions.

Lossy Compression

Significantly reduces file size by lowering image quality and removing metadata. Ideal for image-heavy documents.

Pro Tip: Scanned Documents

If your PDF contains scanned images of text, consider converting them to black and white (grayscale) before compressing. This simple step can often reduce the file size by an additional 50% without affecting readability!
